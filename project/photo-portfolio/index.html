About

For me, photography started out as a hobby. One of my earliest memories is of standing in the Wright Park greenhouse holding our family's giant Nikon film camera, peering through the magical window at the flowers while my dad guided my finger to the shutter. Since then, on family trips, I would always eagerly reach for the camera, liking nothing better than to turn slowly in a circle at some Greek monument while snapping blurry photos of my finger. My first official photography class was in my sophomore year of high school. In all honesty, I chose the class because I am not good at much else in the way of art - science, math, and technology are more of my strong suit. But as I learned to apply some rigor to my preexisting skills as a photographer, I decided that I liked the subject enough to continue pursuing it further.

Since I first saw his work, Ansel Adams has been my favorite photographer. Something about his majestic black-and-white prints appeals to my sense of wonder and speaks volumes about the power of nature. My appreciation for him only grew as I tried my hand at making my own prints in the darkroom, and I have tried to apply his theories of color balance to my own creations. This year especially, I have attempted to become more focused in my process. I have taught myself to pay attention to framing and composition, which allowed me to take more deliberate photos. Though many of my "best" works are still unplanned, I have begun to instinctively move to capture moments in a more professional way. Though I do not believe I will become an artist or a photographer in my career, I firmly believe in being a well-rounded person. After all, what is rationality without love, and emotion without logic? I hope that photography can be something I take with me through the paths of life, one of many lenses through which I will see the world.
